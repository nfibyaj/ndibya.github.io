Quick Unboxing & Quick Review

The Nothing Phone (2) offers a unique design with its transparent back and customizable LED notification lights, known as the Glyph Interface. It features a 6.7-inch AMOLED display with a 120Hz refresh rate, ensuring smooth visuals. Powered by the Snapdragon 8+ Gen 1 processor, it delivers strong performance. The phone boasts a dual-camera setup with a 50MP main sensor and a 50MP ultra-wide sensor, providing good photo and video quality. Running on Nothing OS, it offers a clean, near-stock Android experience. Overall, it's a stylish and capable mid-range smartphone.

Design and Build

The Nothing Phone 2a maintains the distinctive, transparent design aesthetic of its predecessors but with some notable changes. The camera module is now centrally placed in a raised, pill-shaped bump, giving it a retro-robotic look. The phone's body is mostly polycarbonate, making it lightweight and more resistant to shattering compared to glass-backed phones. However, it is more prone to scratches and fingerprints

Performance

Powered by the MediaTek Dimensity 7200 Pro chipset, the Phone 2a performs well for its price range. It handles multitasking and gaming smoothly, although it's not designed for high-end gaming performance. The phone comes with either 8GB or 12GB of RAM, ensuring smooth operation and quick app switching

Camera

The camera setup on the Nothing Phone 2a is designed for simplicity, lacking a telephoto lens and relying on digital cropping for zoom. The main camera performs decently in good lighting conditions, but struggles with video in challenging lighting. The camera app is straightforward but offers an expert mode for more control​

Software

Running on Android 14 with Nothing OS 2.5, the software is minimalistic and visually consistent. It includes a few pre-installed apps and offers three years of Android updates and four years of security updates. Some users may find the monochromatic icons and stripped-down design either a hit or miss depending on personal preference​
